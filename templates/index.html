About the Sanctuary

Umred Pauni Karhandla Wildlife Sanctuary

The Umred-Pauni-Karhandla Wildlife Sanctuary (UPKWS) spans across Pauni Tahsil in Bhandara district and the Umred, Kuhi, and Bhiwapur Talukas of Nagpur district — nestled beautifully along the Wainganga River.

Situated approximately 50 km from Nagpur and 60 km from Bhandara, this pristine habitat is intricately connected to the Tadoba Andhari Tiger Reserve. It lies just 40 km north of Tadoba-Andhari and 50 km southwest of Nagzira Wildlife Sanctuary.

The sanctuary hosts breeding tigers, herds of Gaur, wild dogs, flying squirrels, pangolins, honey badgers, and an extraordinary diversity of bird species — making it one of Maharashtra's most significant conservation treasures.

Flora & Fauna

Wildlife You'll Encounter

From elusive big cats to extraordinary rare species, the sanctuary harbours life at every turn. Every safari is a unique story unfolding in the wild.

Tiger

Apex predator of the sanctuary. Regular sightings of breeding tigers and cubs.

Indian Gaur

Massive herds of Gaur roam the meadows, especially near watering holes.

Leopard

Stealthy and elusive, leopards are spotted on rocky outcrops and tree canopies.

Wild Dogs (Dholes)

Pack-hunting dholes are a rare and thrilling sight in these forests.

Getting Here

How To Reach

✈️

By Flight

Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar International Airport in Nagpur is the nearest airport, approximately 68 km from the sanctuary. Taxis and cabs are readily available for onward travel.

🚂

By Train

Nagpur Railway Station is the closest rail hub, just a 1.5-hour drive away. Regular trains connect Nagpur to major cities across India.

🚌

By Bus

Regular buses run every 30 minutes from Bhandara Bus Stand (5 AM – 8 PM). The sanctuary is approx. 50 km from Nagpur and 60 km from Bhandara by road.
